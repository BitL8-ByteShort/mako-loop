# Launch Readiness Mode

Use this for Product Hunt-style launches, demos, buyer-facing releases, public websites, release notes, and final product passes.

## Outcome

The launch promise is clear, credible, and backed by a working demo path.

## Launch Contract

Write:

`For [buyer], [product] delivers [outcome] through [workflow], proven by [demo/proof].`

If this sentence is weak, the product is not launch-ready.

## Readiness Checklist

- Buyer and pain are specific.
- Main promise is understandable in 10 seconds.
- Demo path works on the live surface or packaged artifact.
- CTA is clear: buy, join waitlist, book, install, try, download, or contact.
- Screenshots/video show the actual product.
- Pricing or next step is not confusing.
- Known incomplete surfaces are hidden or honestly framed.
- Onboarding does not expose setup complexity too early.
- Error states do not embarrass the product.
- The product has a short founder/demo script.

## Product Hunt-Style Pass

Check:

- Name and tagline.
- First screenshot.
- Maker comment or launch story.
- GIF/video/demo path.
- Three strongest benefits.
- Credibility proof.
- FAQ/objection answers.
- What a skeptical commenter will attack.

If current Product Hunt rules, categories, or launch timing matter, verify them from current sources before advising.

## Ship / No-Ship

Ship only if:

- The wedge works.
- The value is visible.
- A buyer can act.
- The rough edges are not in the main path.

No-ship if:

- The product needs explanation to feel valuable.
- The main workflow cannot be demonstrated.
- There are visible dead ends in the launch path.
- The landing page promises more than the app can prove.
