# Product Contract

Use this reference when a project lacks a crisp target.

## One-Sentence Contract

Write the project contract as:

`For [specific user], this product helps them [valuable job] by [one workflow], proven when [observable result].`

Examples:

- `For clinic owners, this product captures missed calls and turns them into booked appointments, proven when a new call produces a lead, follow-up, and calendar event.`
- `For agency operators, this product turns a client request into an approval-ready work item, proven when the client can upload, approve, and see status without support help.`

## Rejection Tests

Reject or re-scope work that:

- Adds a primary nav item without improving the contract.
- Creates settings before the main workflow works.
- Exposes diagnostics as product UI.
- Creates a demo-only path that cannot become production behavior.
- Requires users to understand internal provider state before they can act.
- Improves a page but leaves the workflow broken.

## Wedge Preference

Prefer a narrow, valuable, finished workflow over a wide platform shell. Product Hunt-level quality usually comes from a small promise that feels complete.
